Biblical Accountability vs. Being Judgmental

Knowing the Real Jesus

An 80-Minute Journey Through Scripture

The Dangerous Game of Mistaken Identity

Let me tell you about Sarah's embarrassing mistake...

She was going through a difficult divorce and desperately needed to talk to someone.

Sarah's Search for Help

Her friend had given her the number for "James Smith," a counselor who had helped her through her own marital crisis.

Sarah was nervous but finally worked up the courage to call.

Pouring Out Her Heart

When a man answered and said, "This is James," she poured out her heart.

She shared intimate details about her marriage, her fears about the future, her financial struggles, even her deepest insecurities about whether she was lovable.

The Wrong Number

There was an awkward silence...

Then the man said: "Ma'am, I think you have the wrong number. I'm James Smith the plumber, not James Smith the counselor."

A Simple Mistake

Sarah had just shared her most private struggles with a complete stranger.

Why? Because there are over 31,000 James Smiths in the United States alone, and she had dialed the wrong one.

The Same Mistake We Make with God

We might chuckle at Sarah's mistake, but here's what's not funny:

Many people today approach God the same way.

They pray to "God" and tell Him their deepest needs, but they've never bothered to learn who He actually is.

The Wrong Jesus

They've created a Jesus in their minds based on:

• What feels comfortable

• What they've heard from others

• What fits their lifestyle

But what if they're talking to the wrong Jesus?

A Story That Should Chill Us

Larry and Gary Hall were identical twin brothers who grew up in the same house in Indiana.

Same parents, same upbringing, went to the same schools.

If you saw them from a distance, you couldn't tell them apart.

But Here's Where It Gets Terrifying

Larry Hall became one of America's most prolific serial killers, suspected of murdering dozens of women across multiple states.

Meanwhile, his identical twin brother Gary lived a normal life, held down jobs, and was never suspected of any wrongdoing.

Mistaken Identity Could Cost Your Life

Imagine if you were alone at night and approached by one of these brothers.

From appearance alone, you couldn't tell which one you were facing.

A simple case of mistaken identity could cost you your life.

This Is a Spiritual Warning

Many people today think they know Jesus because they know a few basic facts:

• He was a good teacher

• He loved people

• He died on a cross

As Different as Larry and Gary Hall

The Jesus of Scripture and the Jesus of popular culture are as different as Larry and Gary Hall.

One leads to eternal life; the other leads to eternal death.

The Most Terrifying Words Ever Spoken

Many people will stand before God confident they know Him, only to hear:

"I never knew you"

- Matthew 7:23

The Question We Must Face

Are we following the Jesus of Scripture, or a Jesus of our own imagination?

"I choose to believe the Bible because it is a reliable collection of historical documents written down by eyewitnesses during the lifetime of other eyewitnesses."

- Voddie Baucham

If we're going to claim to know Jesus, we need to know Him as He actually is, not as we wish He were.

Part 1: The Cultural Confusion About "Don't Judge"

The False Teaching That's Destroying the Church

"Jesus said not to judge, so we should never tell anyone they're doing anything wrong."

This idea has become so deeply embedded in our culture that even Christians feel guilty about addressing sin.

This Teaching Is Devastating Lives

When we refuse to lovingly address sin, we're not being loving—we're being negligent.

We're like doctors who refuse to tell patients they have cancer because we don't want to hurt their feelings.

What Jesus Actually Said About Judging

Let's look at the most misquoted passage in the Bible...

"Do not judge, or you too will be judged. For in the same way you judge others, you will be judged, and with the measure you use, it will be measured to you.

Why do you look at the speck of sawdust in your brother's eye and pay no attention to the plank in your own eye? ...

You hypocrite, first take the plank out of your own eye, and then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother's eye."

- Matthew 7:1-5 (NIV)

Notice What Jesus Is Actually Teaching

He's NOT saying, "Never address sin in others."

He's giving us the proper order and heart attitude for doing so.

Jesus assumes we WILL help remove the speck from our brother's eye—but only after we've dealt with our own issues first.

The Critical Difference

Hypocritical judgment says:

"I'm better than you, and I'm going to point out your sin while ignoring my own."

Loving correction says:

"I struggle with sin too, but I care about you enough to help you see what's hurting you and others."

The Heart Behind True Love

If your friend was about to walk off a cliff because they couldn't see the danger, would love keep you silent?

Of course not! Love would compel you to warn them, even if they got angry at you for it.

Love Cannot Remain Silent

When we see someone we care about walking in patterns that are destroying their life, their relationships, and their witness for Christ, love cannot remain silent.

Love warns.

Part 2: The Corinthian Church

When "Love" Becomes Harmful

A Church That Refused to Address Sin

The church in Corinth thought they were being loving and progressive.

They had a situation that would make headlines today: a man was living in a sexual relationship with his stepmother.

A Shocking Situation

Even the pagan culture around them was shocked by this behavior.

But the church? They were proud of how "accepting" and "non-judgmental" they were.

"It is actually reported that there is sexual immorality among you, and of a kind that even pagans do not tolerate: A man is sleeping with his father's wife. And you are proud! Shouldn't you rather have gone into mourning and have put out of your fellowship the man who has been doing this?"

- 1 Corinthians 5:1-2 (NIV)

Can You Hear Paul's Shock?

"Even the pagans wouldn't tolerate this, but you're proud of your tolerance!"

The Corinthians thought their acceptance showed how much they loved this man.

Paul shows them they were actually destroying him.

The Danger of Unchecked Sin

"Don't you know that a little yeast leavens the whole batch of dough? Get rid of the old yeast, so that you may be a new unleavened batch."

- 1 Corinthians 5:6-7 (NIV)

A Simple but Powerful Illustration

When you're making bread, it only takes a little yeast to affect the entire batch of dough.

In the same way, when a church tolerates serious, unrepentant sin, it affects the entire community.

Why Sin Spreads

When we see others getting away with behavior that God clearly calls wrong, it makes it easier for us to justify our own compromises.

Before long, the whole church develops a casual attitude toward things that break God's heart.

The Greater Harm

When we enable someone to continue in sin without consequences, we're not helping them—we're hurting them.

We're making it easier for them to ignore God's voice and walk further away from Him.

Think of It This Way

If your teenager was experimenting with drugs, would love ignore it?

Or would love intervene, even if it meant conflict and consequences?

The Corinthians were treating spiritual danger the same way neglectful parents treat physical danger—by pretending it wasn't really that serious.

"But now I am writing to you that you must not associate with anyone who claims to be a brother or sister but is sexually immoral or greedy, an idolater or slanderer, a drunkard or swindler. Do not even eat with such people.

What business is it of mine to judge those outside the church? Are you not to judge those inside? God will judge those outside. 'Expel the wicked person from among you.'"

- 1 Corinthians 5:11-13 (NIV)

Let's Pause Here

Question for Discussion:

What's your immediate reaction to what Paul just said?

Does this sound harsh? Unloving? Why or why not?

This Sounds Harsh to Our Modern Ears

But notice something important: Paul is not talking about people who struggle with sin and are fighting against it.

He's talking about people who claim to be Christians but are living in open, unrepentant sin and refusing to listen to loving correction.

The Goal Is Never Punishment for Punishment's Sake

It's a wake-up call.

Sometimes people need to experience the consequences of their choices before they'll take them seriously.

"Biblical church discipline is simple obedience to God and a simple confession that we need help. We cannot live the Christian life alone. Our purpose in church discipline is positive for the individual disciplined, for other Christians as they see the real danger of sin, for the health of the church as a whole, and for the corporate witness of the church to those outside."

- John Piper

Paul's Command Was Clear

The church was instructed to remove this man from fellowship.

Not out of hatred, but out of love—for him and for the church.

This is what biblical accountability looks like.

The Beautiful Ending

In 2 Corinthians, we learn that the church discipline worked!

The man repented and was restored to fellowship.

"The punishment inflicted on him by the majority is sufficient. Now instead, you ought to forgive and comfort him, so that he will not be overwhelmed by excessive sorrow. I urge you, therefore, to reaffirm your love for him."

- 2 Corinthians 2:6-8 (NIV)

The Goal Was Always Restoration

Church discipline isn't about punishment—it's about restoration.

When this man hit rock bottom, he turned back to God.

And the church welcomed him back with love and forgiveness.

This is the heart of biblical accountability.

True Love Acts

True love doesn't enable sin.

True love speaks the truth.

True love sets boundaries.

True love pursues restoration.

Part 3: The Jesus Process

Protection for Everyone

Jesus Gave Us a Clear Process

Matthew 18:15-17 outlines a process that protects both the person and the community.

It's brilliant, loving, and often ignored by the modern church.

Step 1: Private Conversation

"If your brother or sister sins, go and point out their fault, just between the two of you. If they listen to you, you have won them over."

- Matthew 18:15 (NIV)

Why Start Private?

This protects the person's dignity and reputation.

Most issues can be resolved at this level if approached with humility and love.

The goal is to "win them over," not to shame them.

Step 2: Bring Witnesses

"But if they will not listen, take one or two others along, so that 'every matter may be established by the testimony of two or three witnesses.'"

- Matthew 18:16 (NIV)

The Purpose of Witnesses

Witnesses provide accountability and protection.

They can confirm the truth of the situation and help the person see the seriousness of the issue.

This step prevents false accusations and provides multiple perspectives.

Step 3: Tell the Church

"If they still refuse to listen, tell it to the church; and if they refuse to listen even to the church, treat them as you would a pagan or a tax collector."

- Matthew 18:17 (NIV)

Only When Necessary

Only after private attempts have failed does the matter become public.

Even then, the church's involvement is meant to help the person see the gravity of their sin.

At every step, the goal is restoration, not condemnation.

Jesus Protects Everyone

This process protects:

• The dignity of the person caught in sin

• The integrity of the accuser

• The purity of the church community

It starts private and only becomes public if absolutely necessary.

"It is one thing for me to claim that God has changed me; it is quite another for those around me to acknowledge that I have truly changed. We are self-deceived. We are in desperate need of brothers and sisters who will tell us the truth."

- Voddie Baucham

Why We Need Each Other

We all have blind spots.

We all justify our behavior.

We all minimize our sin.

We need people who love us enough to tell us the truth.

Part 4: True vs. False Conversion

The Heart of the Matter

What If They're Not Really Saved?

Everything so far assumes we're dealing with true believers.

But what if we're dealing with people who think they're Christians...

But have never actually been born again?

Jesus' Sobering Words

"Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven. Many will say to me on that day, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name and in your name drive out demons and in your name perform many miracles?' Then I will tell them plainly, 'I never knew you. Away from me, you evildoers!'"

- Matthew 7:21-23 (NIV)

Did You Catch That Word "MANY"?

Jesus isn't talking about a few confused people.

MANY people will be shocked to discover they were never truly converted.

These are people who did religious things, even miracles, in Jesus' name—but they never truly knew Him.

Not All "Belief" Is Saving Faith

In Luke 8:11-15, Jesus tells the Parable of the Seeds.

It reveals that there are different types of responses to the Gospel—and not all of them result in salvation.

The Rocky Ground

"Those on the rocky ground are the ones who receive the word with joy when they hear it, but they have no root. They believe for a while, but in the time of testing they fall away."

- Luke 8:13 (NIV)

Key Insight

"They believe for a while, but in the time of testing they fall away."

This person had joy. They even had belief.

But it wasn't real saving faith because it didn't last.

"Jesus said that the number of such self-proclaiming believers, who are not really regenerate, is declared to be 'many.' This should elicit caution in our assumptions about our 'evangelism statistics.'"

- R.C. Sproul

The Badge of True Faith: Endurance

Real faith doesn't just start strong—it endures through difficulties, doubts, and seasons of struggle.

"You need to persevere so that when you have done the will of God, you will receive what he has promised."

- Hebrews 10:36 (NIV)

Think About a Marathon

Anyone can run the first mile with enthusiasm.

But only trained runners finish the race.

In the same way, many people start the Christian life with excitement, but only those with genuine faith keep going when things get difficult.

"They went out from us, but they did not really belong to us. For if they had belonged to us, they would have remained with us; but their going showed that none of them belonged to us."

- 1 John 2:19 (NIV)
"Perseverance is the badge of true saints. The Christian life is not a beginning only in the ways of God, but also a continuance in the same as long as life lasts... Final perseverance is the necessary evidence of genuine conversion."

- Charles Spurgeon

This Doesn't Mean Perfection

True Christians struggle, doubt, and even sin.

We all have seasons of weakness and times when our faith feels dry.

The difference is that genuine believers don't completely abandon faith and permanently return to their old way of life.

They may wander, but they don't completely walk away. They may stumble, but they get back up.

Part 5: The Fruit Test

How Real Faith Shows Itself

True Christianity Bears Fruit

"By their fruit you will recognize them. Do people pick grapes from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? Likewise, every good tree bears good fruit, but a bad tree bears bad fruit."

- Matthew 7:16-17 (NIV)

A Simple but Powerful Principle

You can tell what kind of tree you're looking at by the fruit it produces.

In the same way, you can tell what kind of faith someone has by the fruit it produces in their life.

"In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead."

- James 2:17 (NIV)
"No one who is born of God will continue to sin, because God's seed remains in them; they cannot go on sinning, because they have been born of God. This is how we know who the children of God are and who the children of the devil are."

- 1 John 3:9-10 (NIV)

Warning Signs We Should Take Seriously

• Lack of conviction about sin—they're comfortable with behaviors God clearly calls wrong

• No hunger for God's Word—the Bible feels boring or irrelevant to them

• No desire for fellowship with believers—they prefer the company of unbelievers and avoid church

More Warning Signs

• No fruit of the Spirit over time—their character doesn't reflect love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control

• Easy acceptance of sin in their life—they have explanations for why God's commands don't apply to them

• Defensive attitude when confronted with biblical truth—they get angry rather than humble when corrected

The Broken Compass

A broken compass is dangerous not because it doesn't point somewhere, but because it points confidently in the wrong direction.

False assurance is more dangerous than honest doubt because it keeps people from seeking the truth.

Conclusion

The Life-and-Death Importance of Getting This Right

The Journey We've Taken

We've covered a lot of ground today, but it all comes down to this:

Knowing the real Jesus and living in genuine relationship with Him is a matter of eternal life and death.

Four Essential Truths We Cannot Compromise

1. True Christians cannot overlook sin—love requires addressing it biblically

2. Church discipline is loving—it aims to restore people and protect the community

Four Essential Truths (continued)

3. True faith endures—those who completely fall away likely never truly belonged to Christ

4. Real Christianity produces fruit—genuine conversion shows itself through changed life and perseverance

Always Remember the Goal

The goal is never condemnation but restoration, protection, and the glory of God through holy living.

Remember the Hall Twins

Two men who looked identical on the outside but were completely different on the inside.

Many people today think they know Jesus because they know some facts about Him, but they've never truly met Him as Lord and Savior.

They're following a Jesus of their imagination rather than the Jesus of Scripture.

"Preach the word; be prepared in season and out of season; correct, rebuke and encourage—with great patience and careful instruction."

- 2 Timothy 4:2 (NIV)

Biblical accountability combines correction with encouragement, always motivated by love for God and others.
