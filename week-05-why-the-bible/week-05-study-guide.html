Summary

The Bible is a reliable collection of historical documents written by eyewitnesses during the lifetime of other eyewitnesses; they report supernatural events that took place in fulfillment of specific prophecies and claim that their writings are divine rather than human in origin.

Main Points

  1. The most critical question for any Christian is "Why the Bible?" above other religious texts
  2. Common inadequate answers include "that's how I was raised" and personal experience alone
  3. The Bible is a reliable collection of historical documents from multiple sources
  4. These documents were written by actual eyewitnesses to the events described
  5. The writings occurred during the lifetime of other living eyewitnesses who could verify or falsify claims
  6. The authors report supernatural events, not just moral teachings or philosophy
  7. These supernatural events fulfilled specific, detailed prophecies written centuries earlier
  8. The manuscript evidence for the Bible far exceeds any other ancient document
