üö´ Myth Buster

Separate common myths about forgiveness from biblical truth:
"True forgiveness means forgetting what happened."
MYTH: Humans weren't made to forget. The power of forgiveness is choosing not to punish despite remembering. Forgetting isn't required - cancelling the debt is.
"Forgiveness means cancelling the debt completely."
TRUTH: This is Voddie's core definition. If someone destroys your phone, forgiveness means they don't have to buy you a new one. The debt is cancelled.
"You can only forgive someone who asks for forgiveness."
MYTH: Forgiveness is a lonely highway - you can do it alone. You can even forgive dead people. Reconciliation requires two people, but forgiveness doesn't.
"If you really loved me, you wouldn't sin against me so much."
MYTH: Greater intimacy means seeing more sin, not less sin. If you interact 3 times a day instead of 3 times a month, you'll see more failures. This means YOU need to forgive more, not that THEY need to sin less.
"We punish people by withholding attention, affection, or honor."
TRUTH: This is exactly how we make people pay in relationships - the silent treatment, coldness, withholding respect. True forgiveness stops this punishment.
"I can forgive but I don't have to trust them again."
MYTH & TRUTH: This mixes forgiveness with reconciliation. You CAN forgive without trusting - forgiveness cancels the debt. But rebuilding trust is part of reconciliation, which requires both people working together.
