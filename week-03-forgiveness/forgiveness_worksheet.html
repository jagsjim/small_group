üí∏ Debt Cancellation Simulator

Experience what true forgiveness means through real scenarios:
Scenario: Your friend borrows your brand new phone and accidentally drops it in a lake. It's completely destroyed and would cost $800 to replace. They're devastated and apologizing profusely. You say "I forgive you." What does that actually mean?
"I'm not going to yell at you or be mean, but you definitely need to buy me a new phone."
"It's okay, I won't stay mad at you forever, but this is going to take time to get over."
"The debt is cancelled. You don't owe me anything - not money, not guilt, not payback."

üéØ How Do You Punish?

Voddie says we punish by withholding attention, affection, or honor. Honestly assess your patterns:
Withholding Attention
"I don't want to see you right now. Get out of my face."
I give people the silent treatment when they hurt me
I avoid spending time with people who have disappointed me
Withholding Affection
"You can have my attention, but there will be no warmth from me."
I act cold and distant when someone hurts me
I withhold physical affection (hugs, kisses) to make my point
Withholding Honor
"It's their birthday, but I'm not calling because they don't deserve it."
I refuse to show respect to people who have failed me
I withhold praise or recognition as punishment
Recognizing these patterns is the first step to freedom. We all do this - but Christ calls us to something better.

üìà The Intimacy Paradox

Your spouse/closest person:
Low Need for Forgiveness High Need for Forgiveness
