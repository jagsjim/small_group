Are You Really A Christian? | Paul Washer

Watch on YouTube

Here's a small-group discussion guide based on the Paul Washer sermon transcript provided.

Summary

This sermon challenges listeners to examine their lives and ensure their faith is genuine, marked by a transformed life and a growing desire for holiness.

  • Christianity is more than just a cultural thing or something you do on Sunday; it's a complete transformation of your life.
  • True believers are not saved by works, but their faith will produce a desire to be holy and to turn away from the world.
  • God saves people to demonstrate His power, not because they deserve it, but because He is a Savior.
  • Lowering the standards of the Gospel leads to ungodly people in the church and blasphemes God's name.
  • A truly saved person is a new creation with new affections and desires to serve Christ and be holy.
  • God works in believers to pull them out of the world and draw them closer to Himself.
  • If you love the world and its ways, it might be evidence that God has not done a transforming work in you.
  • The evidence of true conversion is not a one-time prayer but a continuous repentance and transformation.

Overview Questions

  1. What does it mean to you that God's grace doesn't give us permission to do whatever we want?
  2. Do you feel that you may abuse His grace counting on forgiveness?
  3. Does knowing that God's power is in you change the way you view your own struggles and growth as a Christian?
  4. Are there areas in your life where you might be trying to "have God and the world too," and what practical steps can you take this week to separate yourself from those worldly influences?
  5. How do you feel knowing that God's reputation depends on the transformation He brings about in your life?
  6. What's one way our group can help each other stay focused on God and pursue holiness?

Transcript

  1. Introduction: Standing Before God (00:00)
    1. One day each of you will stand before God and be judged.
    2. Some will go to heaven; others will face God's wrath in hell.
    3. Be careful; Christianity is not a cultural thing or a small part of your life.
  2. What is True Christianity? (00:45)
    1. Christianity is not about living in the world six days a week and coming to church on Sunday.
    2. If you are just like the world all the time and then come to church on Sunday, you have no Christianity.
    3. How do you know you are a Christian? How do you know you'll go to heaven?
    4. It is all of grace and not saved by works, but by believing the promises of the Gospel.
  3. Grace and Sanctification (01:42)
    1. Grace gives you the power to continue repenting and believing.
    2. Grace given for justification also provides grace for sanctification.
    3. One of the greatest evidences of true belief in Christ is the work of sanctification in you.
    4. Is your great desire to be holy, not like the world, but like Jesus Christ? If not, be afraid.
  4. God's Motive for Saving People (03:01)
    1. God's motive is not found in people; all have sinned.
    2. Sin only motivates God to judge and condemn.
    3. God saves us in spite of ourselves because He is a Savior.
    4. We do not deserve to be loved; we deserve His wrath. He saves us because He is love.
  5. The Danger of Lowering the Gospel Standard (03:56)
    1. When a church lowers the standard to attract more people, it fills up with ungodly people.
    2. The actions of ungodly people cause the world to blaspheme God's name.
    3. Those who claim to know Christ but live contrary to His word are not Christians.
    4. We are saved by faith alone, not by works, but a truly saved person is a new creation.
  6. Transformation and New Desires (05:02)
    1. God makes them new creatures with new affections and desires to serve Christ and be holy.
    2. Do you long to be like the world? If so, be terrified.
    3. If God's power is not seen in your life leading you to holiness, maybe there is no power of God in your life.
    4. You are not born again; you are not a Christian.
  7. God's Power Demonstrated (05:50)
    1. God saves people to demonstrate how powerful He is in saving their souls and transforming their lives.
    2. Christians are not sinless or perfect, but God is working, teaching, and disciplining them.
    3. Are your desires for Christ and holiness growing?
    4. Are you becoming less like the world and more like Christ?
  8. God's Work in Believers (06:43)
    1. God begins to work in them to pull them out of the world, worldliness, and sin, and bring them to Himself.
    2. Is this obvious in your life?
    3. If you have truly believed, God will be working in you to make you holy.
    4. If there is no evidence of this, there is a good chance you have not been converted.
  9. Separation from the World (07:42)
    1. When God saves, He cuts them off from the world—everything that contradicts God's will.
    2. He separates them, changes their life, and draws them unto Himself.
    3. Holiness means being separated from the world.
  10. Friendship with the World (08:30)
    1. The Scriptures teach us what God hates so we will run from it.
    2. There can be no friendship between the believer and the world.
    3. If God is working in you, He will use His word and Spirit to reveal what is wrong in the world and draw you away.
    4. If you want God, you must let go of the world; if you love the world, the love of the Father is not in you.
  11. The Deceit of the World (09:32)
    1. The culture is full of things that glitter but are not gold and promises that are lies.
    2. It will try to turn you away from Christ, telling you that you can have God and the world.
    3. It is a lie!
    4. This is said to save you from the monster that has killed more people than any tyrant.
  12. Warning and Conclusion (10:18)
    1. If you love the world, be afraid; it could be evidence that God has never worked in you.
    2. If He saves you, He will finish the work He began.
    3. Every Christian is a demonstration of God's power.
    4. Can you prove that since your conversion, God is working to make you holy?
  13. Living Like the World vs. Being Transformed (11:22)
    1. Can you call yourself Christian and yet look, act, talk, and dress like the world?
    2. God will begin to cleanse them when he truly saves a person.
    3. The moment we believe in Jesus Christ, we are justified and right with God through faith.
    4. God will begin a lifetime work of sanctification, changing us and cleansing us from filthiness and idols.
  14. Evidence of Belonging to God (12:25)
    1. One of the evidences that you truly belong to God is that He is involved in your life to make you clean.
    2. If you are converted, God has a claim on you and will change you for His glory and love.
    3. He will not let you stay the way you were and has the power to change you.
    4. If you can live in sin and worldliness without conviction or discipline, it is evidence that you are not truly a child of God.
  15. God's Goal for Your Life (13:27)
    1. God's goal is not prosperity, health, or wealth, but to make you holy and conform you to the image of Christ.
    2. He will cleanse you from your filthiness and idols.
    3. Is He doing that in your life?
    4. The evidence is not a one-time prayer but a continuous repentance.
  16. New Creation (14:22)
    1. The evidence is not just one-time salvation but continuous believing and transformation.
    2. If any man be in Christ, he is a new creature.
    3. Have you become a new creature?
    4. When you fall into sin, does it break your heart, or do you love it?
  17. Transformation of Desires (15:03)
    1. By nature, we love sin, but when converted, God changes us into a new creation with new desires.
    2. We can no longer stomach the sin of this world and are ashamed we ever participated in it.
    3. We begin to walk as saints, not as sinners.
    4. Has God changed your heart, and does He continue changing it? Do you long to be free from the filth of this world and to be like Christ?
    5. If you can say yes, that is a great evidence that you have been born again.
