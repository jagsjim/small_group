Discussion Questions

After Video - Reflect Together
Jesus Christ is Prophet, Priest and King. How does scripture reveal Jesus as the ultimate Prophet? How does he still function in the office of a prophet today in our lives?
The disciples listened to Jesus for three years, yet still needed him to 'open their minds' to the Scriptures after his resurrection. Why do we need this same divine help to understand God's Word, and how have you experienced that need recently?
Can you describe the time in your life when the spiritual cataracts first fell off your eyes? What was that like?
The sermon describes three signs that Jesus is opening your heart: freshness, delight, and authority. Which of these three do you see most in your life right now? Which one feels like it's missing?
What's one small step you could take this week to invite Jesus the Prophet to speak more clearly into your life?

Key Truth

Jesus is not only the ultimate prophet who speaks God's truth - He IS the Living Truth who opens our blind eyes to understand and delight in Him.
1
What is a prophet? A prophet is God's spokesman who communicates divine revelation - not just foretelling the future, but speaking God's authoritative truth to His people.
2
Jesus is a prophet As the Son of God, Jesus has always been THE Prophet - speaking and revealing God's will from creation through the incarnation and continuing today.
3
Jesus is a prophet like Moses Both were endangered as babies, tested in the desert, covenant mediators, brought God's Word, performed miracles, and delivered God's people - but Jesus is infinitely superior.
4
Jesus is the unique prophet Unlike other prophets who only communicated God's content, Jesus originates content as God - He IS the Living Word, the Truth itself, and the One who determines the future.
5
We need Jesus to open our minds Even after three years with Jesus, the disciples couldn't understand Scripture until Jesus "opened" (dianoigo) their minds - like removing cataracts that we can't remove ourselves.
6
Jesus brings illumination through understanding He assembles the puzzle pieces of Scripture so we can "get it" - connecting content we knew externally into internal understanding that transforms us.
7
Three signs Jesus is opening your heart Freshness: Scripture never gets old; familiar passages suddenly reveal new truth. Delight: The Word produces joy in obedience, not just duty. Authority: God's truth produces power for actual change and transformation.
8
Jesus continues this prophetic work today Through the Holy Spirit and the preaching of His Word, Jesus still actively opens minds, defeats strongholds, and reveals Himself so we can be saved and grow.
