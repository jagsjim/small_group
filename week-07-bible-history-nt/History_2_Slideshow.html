Joshua Leads a New Generation

Joshua: Finally Entering! üèûÔ∏è

After Moses dies, God speaks to Joshua: "Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go."

After 40 years of wandering, the new generation is ready. God parts the Jordan River (just like the Red Sea!), and they finally enter the land God promised Abraham 500 years earlier!

6/87

The Walls of Jericho üé∫

The first city they face is Jericho, with massive walls. God's battle plan? March around the city once a day for six days. On the seventh day, march seven times, blow trumpets, and shout!

When they obey, the walls collapse! This shows that victory comes not through human strength but through trusting and obeying God. The conquest of Canaan has begun.

7/87

But They Don't Fully Obey...

God commanded them to drive out all the Canaanites because of their wickedness and idolatry. But the Israelites get comfortable and leave many Canaanites in the land.

This partial obedience will haunt them for centuries. The remaining Canaanites will lead them into idolatry again and again.

‚öñÔ∏è The Judges: Chaos and Cycles

When Everyone Does What's Right in Their Own Eyes

9/87

The Depressing Cycle üîÑ

After Joshua dies, Israel has no central leader. What follows is 300+ years of chaos. The book of Judges records this cycle repeated seven times:

  1. Israel does evil and worships idols
  2. God allows enemies to oppress them
  3. Israel cries out to God
  4. God raises up a judge to deliver them
  5. Peace while the judge lives
  6. The judge dies, cycle repeats
10/87

Moral Chaos

"In those days Israel had no king; everyone did as they saw fit."
- Judges 21:25

The book ends with horrific stories of violence, idolatry, and moral confusion. Israel has become as bad as the Canaanites they were supposed to drive out. They desperately need a king.

üëë The Kings of Israel

Human Kings Can't Save

12/87

Israel Demands a King

The people come to Samuel the prophet: "Give us a king to lead us, such as all the other nations have." This breaks God's heart - HE is supposed to be their king! But they want to be like everyone else.

God warns them that a human king will take their sons for his army, their daughters for his palace, their best fields, and tax them heavily. But they insist: "We want a king!"

13/87

Saul: The King Who Failed

God gives them Saul - tall, handsome, impressive. He starts well but soon reveals his true heart. He partially obeys God's commands, makes excuses, and cares more about people's opinions than God's.

Samuel's response: "To obey is better than sacrifice... Because you have rejected the word of the Lord, he has rejected you as king."

14/87

A Shepherd Boy Becomes King

God sends Samuel to Jesse's house to anoint the next king. Jesse parades seven impressive sons before Samuel, but God rejects them all. "The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart."

Finally, they call in the youngest - David, who's out watching sheep. When Samuel sees him, God says, "Rise and anoint him; this is the one." David is "a man after God's own heart."

15/87

The Giant-Slayer

Before becoming king, David faces Goliath - a 9-foot giant. While trained soldiers cower, this teenage shepherd boy declares: "You come against me with sword and spear... but I come against you in the name of the Lord Almighty!"

David kills Goliath with a single stone. This pictures how Jesus, the son of David, would defeat our greatest enemy with what seemed like weakness.

16/87

God's Covenant with David üìú

After David becomes king and brings peace to Israel, God makes an incredible promise:

"Your house and your kingdom will endure forever before me; your throne will be established forever."
- 2 Samuel 7:16

How can a human throne last forever? This is another promise pointing to Jesus, the eternal King!

17/87

Even David Falls

David, the man after God's heart, commits terrible sins - adultery with Bathsheba and murder of her husband. When confronted, David repents deeply (read Psalm 51), and God forgives him.

But there are consequences. His family is torn apart by violence and rebellion. Even the best human king is deeply flawed. We need a perfect King.

18/87

Solomon: Wisdom and Splendor

David's son Solomon becomes king. When God offers him anything, Solomon asks for wisdom. God is pleased and gives him wisdom, plus riches and honor.

Solomon builds the magnificent temple in Jerusalem. When it's dedicated, God's glory fills the temple so powerfully that the priests can't even stand to minister!

19/87

Solomon's Heart Turns Away

Despite his wisdom, Solomon marries 700 wives and 300 concubines - many from nations God had forbidden. They turn his heart after other gods. The wisest man who ever lived becomes a fool through disobedience.

God announces judgment: "I will tear the kingdom away from you."

20/87

The Kingdom Splits in Two

After Solomon dies, his son Rehoboam foolishly threatens to make the people's burden even heavier. Ten tribes rebel and form the northern kingdom (Israel). Only Judah and Benjamin remain loyal to David's line.

From this point on, God's people are divided. Most kings are wicked. The northern kingdom doesn't have a single good king!

In 722 BC, after centuries of idolatry, the northern kingdom of Israel is conquered by Assyria. The ten tribes are scattered among the nations and largely lost to history.

This is God's judgment for breaking the covenant. But what about Judah in the south?

23/87

Babylon Destroys Jerusalem

Judah lasts longer and has some good kings who bring revival. But eventually they too fall into idolatry. In 586 BC, Nebuchadnezzar of Babylon conquers Jerusalem.

The unthinkable happens: The temple - God's dwelling place - is destroyed. The ark disappears. The city walls are torn down. The best and brightest are carried off to Babylon. It seems like God's promises have failed.

Isaiah sees a servant who will suffer and die for the people's sins. This doesn't match their expectations of a conquering Messiah!

They rebuild the temple, but it's not as glorious. They rebuild the walls, but they're still under foreign rule. There's no king on David's throne. Something greater is still needed.

  • Abrahamic: How will all nations be blessed?
  • Mosaic: Who can keep the law perfectly?
  • Davidic: Where is the eternal king?

God must have a plan to fulfill them all...

29/87

They're trying to keep the law, waiting for the Messiah, longing for freedom. Many expect a political liberator to overthrow Rome. The stage is set for God's ultimate answer to the problem of sin...
