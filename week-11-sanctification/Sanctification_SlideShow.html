Segment 1

Understanding Sanctification

How God lovingly transforms His children

7/68

Let Me Start with a Story...

Imagine you adopt a child who's been living on the streets.

The moment you sign those papers, that child is legally yours—fully part of your family.

8/68

But They Still Have Street Habits...

They might hide food in their room

They might flinch when you move too quickly

They might struggle to trust your love

9/68

But Over Time...

As they experience your consistent care, they begin to change.

They stop hoarding food.
They relax in your presence.
They learn what it means to be loved.

10/68

That's Sanctification!

Justification:
When God saves us, we're instantly His children—complete!

Sanctification:
God's patient, loving work of transforming us to actually live like the children we already are

11/68

We Still Have...

Old patterns 🔄

Old wounds 💔

Old ways of thinking 🧠

But God is patient with us!

12/68
"The only sin we can fight against successfully is a forgiven sin... Justification is the foundation of sanctification."
- John Piper

In other words: We don't change to become God's children—we change because we already are.

13/68

Let Me Share Some Scripture

"And we all, who with unveiled faces contemplate the Lord's glory, are being transformed into his image with ever-increasing glory, which comes from the Lord, who is the Spirit."
2 Corinthians 3:18
14/68

Notice Something Crucial

"are being transformed"

Not "are transforming ourselves"

We're not passive, but God is doing the transforming.

We're contemplating His glory, He's doing the work!

15/68

Think of It Like Learning Guitar 🎸

When you first pick up a guitar, your fingers don't naturally know where to go.

It feels awkward, even painful.

But with practice, guided by a teacher, something amazing happens...

Eventually, your fingers move without thinking. Music just flows!

16/68
"Sanctification is the work of God's free grace, whereby we are renewed in the whole man after the image of God, and are enabled more and more to die unto sin and live unto righteousness."
- R.C. Sproul
17/68

Three Key Truths About How This Works

Let's unpack each one...

18/68

1. It's God's Work, Not Ours

"For it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose."
Philippians 2:13

We're not passive, but God is the primary actor.
He's the sculptor; we're the marble.

19/68

2. It Happens Through Union with Christ

"For if we have been united with him in a death like his, we will certainly also be united with him in a resurrection like his."
Romans 6:5

Because we're connected to Jesus, His life flows into ours.
Like a branch connected to a vine.

20/68

3. It's a Process, Not an Event

"Being confident of this, that he who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus."
Philippians 1:6

Unlike justification (instant), sanctification takes our whole lifetime.

21/68

Why Understanding This Matters

Many Christians live in one of two ditches:

⬅️ Ditch 1: Trying to earn God's love through good behavior
(That's exhausting and impossible!)

➡️ Ditch 2: Since we're saved by grace, how we live doesn't matter
(That's cheap grace—not biblical!)

22/68
"True grace is of a transforming nature... The grace of God in the heart is a holy seed that will certainly grow up to eternal life."
- Jonathan Edwards

The balance: Grace transforms us!

23/68

Let's Pause and Talk

Choose one question that speaks to you:

  • "When you were growing up, did you ever feel like you had to be 'good enough' for your parents? How is God different?"
  • "What's one thing you do just because you love someone, not because you have to?"

Segment 2

The Heart of Obedience

Love as the root, not the fruit

25/68

Let's Return to That Adopted Child

Remember our adopted child?

Once they truly understand they're safe and loved, something beautiful happens...

26/68

They Start Following Family Rules

Not because they have to earn their place

But because they trust their parents

And want to please them

27/68

They Help with Chores

Not to become part of the family

But because they already are

That's the heart of biblical obedience!

28/68
"Our love will be shown by obedience, not by how good we feel about God at a given moment. The Gospel produces obedience. If you love Him, you will keep His commandments."
- Voddie Baucham
29/68

Look at What Jesus Himself Said

"If you love me, keep my commands."
John 14:15
30/68

Notice What Jesus DIDN'T Say

❌ He didn't say:
"Keep my commands so that I'll love you"

✅ He said:
"If you love me, keep my commands"

The love comes first, then obedience naturally follows!

31/68

One of the Most Misunderstood Passages

Let's read it carefully...

32/68
"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast. For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."
Ephesians 2:8-10
33/68

See the Order?

Verses 8-9: Saved by grace ✨

Verse 10: Created for good works 🛠️

Works don't produce salvation;
Salvation produces works!

34/68
"Be killing sin, or sin will be killing you."
- John Owen

But notice—he's talking to people who are already Christians.

We fight sin not to become God's children, but because we are.

35/68

The Freedom of Gospel Obedience

When you understand that obedience flows from love rather than fear, everything changes.

You're not constantly wondering:
"Have I done enough?"

Instead, you're asking:
"How can I express my gratitude?"

36/68

Think About It This Way...

If someone rescued you from a burning building 🔥

You wouldn't spend the rest of your life trying to pay them back

37/68

But You Might...

  • Send them a card every year 💌
  • Name your child after them 👶
  • Tell everyone about their heroism 📢

That's gratitude, not payment!

38/68
"Good actions are not the root; they are the fruit of a changed heart."
- C.S. Lewis

He understood: Transformation first, then action.

39/68

Let's Reflect Together

"How does understanding obedience as gratitude rather than payment change your daily walk with God?"

Take a moment to think before sharing...

Segment 3

The Fruit of the Spirit

Natural growth, not manufactured results

41/68

Here's Where Many of Us Get Tripped Up

We read Galatians 5:22-23 like it's a to-do list:

42/68
"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law."
Galatians 5:22-23
43/68

The Wrong Approach

📅 "Okay, today I'm going to be more joyful"

📅 "Tomorrow I'll work on patience"

📅 "Next week: kindness bootcamp!"

But that's like tying apples onto a dead tree and calling it alive! 🍎🌳

44/68

Jesus Gives Us a Better Picture

45/68
"Remain in me, as I also remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself; it must remain in the vine. Neither can you bear fruit unless you remain in me. I am the vine; you are the branches. If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing."
John 15:4-5
46/68

Notice What Jesus DOESN'T Say

❌ "Try really hard to produce fruit"

❌ "Have fruit-producing meetings"

❌ "Set fruit production goals"

✅ He says: "Remain in me"

47/68

REMAIN

The fruit is the natural result of staying connected to Him

48/68

Think About a Healthy Apple Tree 🍎🌳

It doesn't strain to produce apples

It doesn't have apple-producing meetings

It doesn't set apple goals for the quarter

It simply draws nutrients from the soil,
receives sunshine and rain,
and naturally produces fruit.

That's its nature!

49/68
"A bad tree cannot bear good fruit. This is why we need to be made new creatures, with new hearts... Out of the heart that has been made new comes the fruit of obedience."
- R.C. Sproul
50/68

If You're Not Seeing Growth...

In love, joy, peace, and the other fruit...

The solution isn't to try harder

The solution is to examine your connection to the vine

51/68

Ask Yourself...

  • Am I spending time in God's Word, letting it nourish my soul? 📖
  • Am I talking to God in prayer, maintaining that vital connection? 🙏
  • Am I in fellowship with other believers who encourage my growth? 👥
  • Am I confessing sin quickly, removing barriers between me and God? ✨
52/68

An Important Truth

A lack of spiritual fruit isn't a call to try harder

It's a call to draw closer

53/68

This Brings Us to James's Challenge

54/68
"In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead."
James 2:17
"As the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without deeds is dead."
James 2:26
55/68

James Isn't Contradicting Paul

He's saying that real faith—living faith—naturally produces works

Just like a living tree naturally produces fruit

If there's no fruit over time, we need to ask whether the tree is truly alive

56/68
"The degree of love to God may be known by the degree of our hatred of sin."
- Jonathan Edwards

Real transformation shows itself in real change.
