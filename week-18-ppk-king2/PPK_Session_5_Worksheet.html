Part 4: Tap to Learn

Tap each card to reveal the answer

What are the two components of a stronghold?
1. Arguments - False belief systems that seem logical but contradict God's truth

2. Pretensions - Pride and self-will that refuses to submit to God's authority
What does it mean to "take every thought captive to obey Christ"?
When a thought comes that doesn't align with God's truth, we capture it and examine it. We ask: "Is this thought true according to God's word? Does it lead me to trust Jesus or something else?" We don't let false thoughts run free and rule us.
What was the main lesson from the Blondin tightrope story?
We must put ALL our trust in Jesus and stop trying to "balance ourselves" or maintain control. Just as Colcord had to become "one with Blondin" to make it across safely, we must surrender completely to Jesus and trust Him with everything.
Why did the widow's two coins mean more than the rich people's large gifts?
She gave "everything she had to live on" (Greek: bios - her whole life). Money represents control, and she gave up what little control she had. This passage is primarily about trust and relinquishing control, not just about financial giving.
