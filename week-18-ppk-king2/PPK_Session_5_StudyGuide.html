SESSION 5: PROPHET, PRIEST, KING (5 of 5)

The King Who Rescues and Rules Our Hearts

WATCH VIDEO
NOTE ABOUT THIS SESSION

This session combines segments from two related sermons on King Jesus. We've woven them together to give you the fullest picture of how Jesus rescues us from false kings and establishes His loving rule in our hearts. We apologize that the transitions may not be completely seamless, but we believe the combined message will be powerful and encouraging.

SUMMARY

This session explores how we were created to live under King Jesus, but our hearts naturally seek false kings instead. Jesus wages a rescue mission against the lies and strongholds that enslave us, using His word to demolish false beliefs and our pride. Through humbling circumstances and the power of His truth, He frees us to experience abundant life under His gentle, perfect rule. The session concludes by showing how Jesus is uniquely both the righteous King who demands obedience and the merciful High Priest who forgives our disobedience, calling us to trust Him completely with everything.
God made you to live under King Jesus, and since you will inevitably serve whatever you seek first, He wages war against false kings in your heart through His word and humbling circumstances to set you free for abundant life in Him.

MAIN POINTS

  1. We were created to live under the perfect rule of King Jesus, not to rule ourselves.
  2. Whatever you seek after first, you will inevitably end up serving—this is part of the human condition.
  3. Good things become false kings when we make them ultimate things, and false kings always disappoint.
  4. Jesus rescues us by waging war against the lies and false beliefs that keep us from seeing His worth.
  5. King Jesus comes with meekness and gentleness, using His word as the weapon to demolish strongholds.
  6. He targets both our false arguments (wrong belief systems) and our pretensions (pride and self-will).
  7. God uses humbling circumstances and trials to bring down our pride so we can see how much we need Him.
  8. The kingship of Jesus in our life is directly proportional to the word of Jesus in our life.

So when we come to God's word we kind of have this filter on, a cultural filter if you will, that screens some of the absolute nature of the claims of Christ and the message of Christianity—one being the absolute kingship of Jesus Christ.
