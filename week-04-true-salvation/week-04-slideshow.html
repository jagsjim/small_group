An 80-Minute Teaching Session

True Salvation

Why This Topic Matters

Today we're diving into one of the most important topics you'll ever encounter: true salvation

What We'll Cover Today

Our Journey Through Salvation

  • The problem of sin
  • How God's law reveals that problem
  • God's just response
  • Christ's rescue plan
  • Our reconciliation and new life

A Key Quote

"You contribute nothing to your salvation except the sin that made it necessary."
— Jonathan Edwards

This might sound harsh, but it's actually the most freeing truth you'll ever hear.

What Is Salvation?

Salvation means being rescued and reconciled to God

By the end, you'll understand:

  • Why we can't earn our way to God
  • Why Christ had to come
  • What it means to live as someone who's been truly saved

The Problem: Sin and Total Depravity

Every person is born alienated from God—"dead in sin"—and unable to restore that relationship on our own.

The Hard Truth

The Bible doesn't sugarcoat our condition:

Romans 3:23:"for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."
Ephesians 2:1:"As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins."

Dead, Not Sick

Notice that word "dead"

  • Not sick
  • Not injured
  • Spiritually dead

A dead person can't help themselves.

What Is Transgression?

Transgression = rebellion against God's commands

The Problem Goes Deeper:

  • We don't just do bad things occasionally
  • Our very hearts are skewed away from God
  • We don't just do wrong things; we are sinners by nature

Quotes on Sin

"Original sin has marred our nature. We are depraved."
— R.C. Sproul
"Men love darkness… We love darkness rather than light" (John 3:19)
— John Piper

We prefer hiding from God rather than walking in His light.

Adam's Fall

Romans 5:12:"sin entered the world through one man… and death came to all people, because all sinned."

Because of Adam's choice:

  • Every human inherits a sinful nature
  • One man's act made many sinners
  • We're born with hearts that naturally rebel against God
  • This is called "total depravity"

God's First Promise

After Adam and Eve sinned, God clothed them with animal skins (Genesis 3:21)

This required the death of innocent animals—the very first sacrifice.

Beautiful truth: This was God's way of pointing forward to the ultimate sacrifice: Christ dying on the cross for our sins.

Our "Good" Works

"But what about all the good things I do?"

Isaiah 64:6:"All of us have become like one who is unclean, and all our righteous acts are like filthy rags; we all shrivel up like a leaf, and like the wind our sins sweep us away."

Even our best moral efforts are insufficient before a holy God.

More Biblical Evidence - Sin

1 John 1:8:"If we claim to be without sin, we deceive ourselves"
Romans 3:10:"There is no one righteous, not even one"
Romans 5:19:"For just as through the disobedience of the one man the many were made sinners..."

Discussion Questions

"If everyone is born spiritually dead, what hope do we have?

Why can't we just try to be good people?"

The Law's Purpose

God didn't give us the law because He expected us to follow it perfectly. Instead, He gave it to reveal our sinfulness.

What Is "The Law"?

God's moral commands:

  • Ten Commandments
  • Moral instructions throughout Scripture
  • Given to show us how we should live

Key insight: The law's primary purpose isn't to save us; it's to show us our need for a Savior.

The Ten Commandments

  1. You shall have no other gods before me.
  2. You shall not make for yourself an idol.
  3. You shall not misuse the name of the Lord your God.
  4. Remember the Sabbath day by keeping it holy.
  5. Honor your father and your mother.
  6. You shall not murder.
  7. You shall not commit adultery.
  8. You shall not steal.
  9. You shall not give false testimony against your neighbor.
  10. You shall not covet.

The Law's True Purpose

Romans 3:20: "Therefore no one will be declared righteous in God's sight by the works of the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of our sin."

The law reveals our sinfulness—it doesn't produce salvation.

The Mirror Analogy

Think of the law like a mirror

When you look in a mirror:

  • It has the power to show you dirt on your face
  • But it can't clean you
  • Only a bath can actually wash away the dirt

Spiritual Mirror

Similarly, God's law acts as a spiritual mirror

The law:

  • Reveals our moral failures and need for cleansing
  • Has no power to make us clean
  • Shows us the standard
  • Reveals how far we fall short
  • Points us to our need for a Savior

More on the Law's Purpose

Galatians 3:24: "So the law was our guardian until Christ came that we might be justified by faith."
Romans 7:7: "What shall we say, then? Is the law sinful? Certainly not! Nevertheless, I would not have known what sin was had it not been for the law."

God's Justice & Hell

Because God is holy and just, sin cannot go unpunished.

Why Can't God Just Overlook Sin?

Romans 6:23: "For the wages of sin is death..."
Matthew 25:46: "Then they will go away to eternal punishment, but the righteous to eternal life."

Wages of sin is death = spiritual death (separation from God) and eventual physical death

Love & Justice Meet

God's love and justice aren't contradictory

They meet perfectly at the cross.

A Loving Judge

A loving judge doesn't let criminals go free.

Revelation 20:15: "Anyone whose name was not found written in the book of life was thrown into the lake of fire."

Discussion Question

If you were a judge and someone you loved committed a crime, how would you balance love and justice?

The Solution: Christ's Rescue Plan

This is where the gospel gets beautiful.

God didn't leave us hopeless.

Incarnation & Sinless Life

God Himself came to earth, lived a perfect life, and qualified to be our Substitute.

The Prophecy Fulfilled

Isaiah 9:6: "For to us a child is born... He will be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God..."
Luke 2:11: "Today... a Savior has been born to you; he is the Messiah, the Lord."

Fully God, Fully Man

Jesus is fully God ("Mighty God") and fully man.

By becoming a baby:

  • He identified with us
  • He could live on our behalf
  • But here's the crucial part—He had to live perfectly

The Sinless Life

Hebrews 4:15 (NIV): "For we do not have a high priest who is unable to empathize with our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are—yet he did not sin."

Jesus lived the sinless life we couldn't live.

What Is "Righteous"?

Being righteous = being perfectly in right standing with God

This means:

  • Never breaking a command
  • Perfect obedience to God's law

A Powerful Thought

"Christ lived the life we should have lived and died the death we should have died."
— Charles Spurgeon

Scripture on Christ's Sinless Life

2 Corinthians 5:21: "God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God."
1 Peter 2:22: "He committed no sin, and no deceit was found in his mouth."
John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us."

Discussion Questions

If your friend offered to pay your debt but was also broke, would that help you?

If someone who had never perfectly obeyed God died, it wouldn't satisfy God's justice—Jesus had to be righteous to stand in our place.

Atonement & Propitiation

Jesus' death on the cross is the payment for sin—He satisfied God's wrath. Our works cannot pay that debt.

The Atoning Sacrifice

1 John 2:2: "He is the atoning sacrifice for our sins, and not only for ours but also for the sins of the whole world."
Romans 3:25-26: "God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness..."

The Currency Analogy

Good works can't save us - we're using the wrong currency

The Airport Store

Imagine returning from Japan with thousands of dollars worth of yen.

You stop at an airport store to buy a $2 bottle of water

When you try to pay with yen, the cashier refuses.

God's Currency System

The same principle applies spiritually

Good works, no matter how valuable they may seem, are the wrong currency for salvation.

Grace and Faith

Ephesians 2:8-9: "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God— not by works, so that no one can boast."

Grace is the "currency" God accepts. Faith is our "hand" that receives the gift.

Rich in Mercy

Ephesians 2:4-5: "But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions."

More on Christ's Sacrifice - Atonement

1 Peter 2:24: "He himself bore our sins in his body on the cross, so that we might die to sins and live for righteousness..."
Romans 5:8: "But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

Discussion Question

Why didn't the Japanese yen work at the airport store?

What does this teach us about good works?

Resurrection

Because Jesus rose from the dead, our salvation is secured; death is defeated.

The Crucial Truth

1 Corinthians 15:3-4: "For what I received I passed on to you as of first importance: that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, that he was buried, that he was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures."

Proof of Payment

The resurrection proves that God accepted Christ's payment for sin.

If He had not risen, the debt would not be paid.

Resurrection

Romans 6:4: "We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life."

Discussion Question

If Christ's death was the payment for sin, what does His resurrection tell us about whether God accepted that payment?

Death Was Arrested

Song: "Death Was Arrested" — Watch on YouTube

The Result

Reconciliation & New Life

Once Christ has paid for our sins, we are declared righteous and brought back into fellowship with God.

Justified by Faith

Romans 3:28: "For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from the works of the law."
2 Corinthians 5:18: "All this is from God, who reconciled us to himself through Christ and gave us the ministry of reconciliation."

What Is Eternal Life?

John 17:3: "Now this is eternal life: that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you have sent."

Key Concepts Defined

Justified

God declares us righteous—He credits Christ's perfect obedience and payment to our account.

Reconciliation

We were God's enemies; now we are His children. The barrier of sin is removed.

New Life

We're not just "forgiven"—we're born again.

Seated with Christ

Ephesians 2:6: "And God raised us up with Christ and seated us with him in the heavenly realms in Christ Jesus."

We have a new identity and position with God.

New Life

2 Corinthians 5:17: "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!"
Romans 8:1: "Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus."

Discussion Questions

After being adopted into a loving family, do you obey to earn your way into the family or because you're grateful for their loving gift?

How does grace change our motivation?

Recap

We saw that every person is dead in sin—total depravity—and no amount of good works can pay the debt of our rebellion.

God gave the Law to show us our need, not to save us.

Closing Prayer

"Dear Heavenly Father, thank You for Your amazing grace. We confess that we are sinners who deserve Your wrath, but You sent Jesus to live, die, and rise for us. Help us rest in Christ alone for our salvation and live in light of this truth. In Jesus' name, Amen."

Final Thought

"You contribute nothing to your salvation except the sin that made it necessary."
— Jonathan Edwards

Questions & Discussion

Let's open the floor for any questions:

  • Is any part of the plan—Our sin problem, Christ's work, or new life—unclear?
  • How has seeing grace vs. works changed your perspective?
  • What questions do you still have about salvation?
